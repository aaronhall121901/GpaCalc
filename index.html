How the GPA calculator works

Know exactly how letter grades translate into grade point average before grades are final.

1. Enter or import your courses

Add every lecture, lab, or elective you want to track. The calculator accepts unlimited rows and respects fractional credit hours so you can mirror any transcript or degree audit.

2. Adjust credits and letter grades

Credits drive the weight of each course in the GPA formula. Update letter grades to instantly recalculate quality points using the fixed 4.0 scale, including plus and minus variations.

3. Review the running summary

The summary panel highlights your cumulative GPA, attempted credits, and total quality points. Use it to test best-case senior-year plans, transfer evaluations, or academic recovery paths.

GPA calculator FAQ

Top search questions answered for students comparing GPA tools.

Is this GPA calculator accurate for college transcripts?
Yes. The math uses the standard four-point grading scale with support for plus/minus grades so it mirrors what most U.S. colleges publish. Always confirm if your institution applies custom weights for honors or AP classes.
Can I track multiple semesters?
Absolutely. Create separate rows for each term, or export your current plan to a spreadsheet when you need to archive it. Nothing is stored on the server, which keeps your data private.
How are quality points calculated?
We multiply the credit hours by the numeric value of each letter grade. For example, 3 credits of a B+ course contribute 9.9 quality points (3 credits × 3.3 points).
Does this tool work for high school GPAs?
Yes, as long as your school reports on the 4.0 scale. If you need weighted AP or IB calculations, enter the adjusted grade values your counselor provides.
